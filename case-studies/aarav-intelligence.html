AARAV Fragrances — Economic Intelligence

Economic intelligence and pitch deck for one of India's top fragrance companies — CSDDD pressure, allergen regulation, and a 2,500-ton expansion

DeliveredIndustry ResearchRegulatory EconomicsCSDDDFragrance Industry

What It Was

AARAV Fragrances & Flavors is one of India's top three fragrance companies by revenue — heavy European export exposure, a newly commissioned 2,500-ton ingredient plant, and a founder with 40 years in the industry. They are facing two regulatory moments simultaneously: the EU CSDDD (Corporate Sustainability Due Diligence Directive) coming into force for their European buyers, and an expanded EU allergen declaration requirement that changes how their entire product catalog is documented for export.

This project was economic intelligence: understanding the specific regulatory timeline, the cost structure of compliance failure (a rejected shipment, a lost EU buyer relationship), and how the new plant expansion creates both exposure and opportunity in this context. The output was a 10-slide pitch deck using economic framing — not "AI will help you," but "here is what it costs when the allergen declaration for one product is wrong, and here is the system that makes that impossible."

The Lesson

Outreach to large companies in regulated industries works when it leads with economic stakes, not technology capabilities. The subject line for the AARAV email: "when the same fragrance needs 3 different answers and what comes next." That framing came from understanding the allergen regulatory landscape well enough to name the specific problem a flavor company faces when EU, UK, and Indian regulations diverge on the same ingredient. Generic AI pitches don't get that specific. Economic intelligence does.
