Sustainability Intelligence — Norex Flavours

Three system designs for India's only MSME in the fragrance sector with EcoVadis Gold — and 5,000 mint farmers in Western UP

In Discussion Agricultural EconomicsEcoVadis Living IncomeSustainabilityCarbon Accounting

Context

Norex Flavours is a Noida-based fragrance and flavor company — the only Indian MSME in the F&F sector to have achieved EcoVadis Gold. They source from approximately 5,000 mint farmers in Western Uttar Pradesh (Barabanki, Badayu, Amroha). They have a Swiss regenerative agriculture pilot underway with a 10-farmer cohort.

Next year they move from the MSME category to the large company category on EcoVadis. That transition triggers a significantly expanded scope: new questions, higher scrutiny, and a new requirement — living income disclosure for all farmers they work with. They are the first company in their category to face this at scale in India.

The Three Problems

After a discovery call with their sustainability manager, three specific pain points became clear — each with a distinct economic structure:

  • Carbon Scope Calculator — Scope 1, 2, and 3 emissions calculated manually every year. Raw data exists in spreadsheets, but emission factors change annually and the calculation is done by hand. They need a system that auto-updates emission factors and calculates from raw inputs, not a static template.
  • Living Income Module — EcoVadis large company category requires disclosing whether the farmers they work with earn a living income. The problem: UP state government has not published a living income benchmark for agricultural workers. No methodology exists to use off-the-shelf. A living income calculation had to be designed from scratch using household expenditure data.
  • Kisaan Diary Digitization — The Swiss regenerative pilot tracks 10 farmers across three crops annually (mint → paddy → potato). Currently: a paper diary. Data needed: fertilizer type, quantity, cost; yield per acre; soil test results (nitrogen, carbon, organic carbon) at year start. Year-over-year comparison for the Swiss partner requires a digital system with report generation.

The Living Income Problem

UP state government has not calculated a living income benchmark for agricultural workers. The methodology had to be designed before any number could be calculated. This is not a data engineering problem. It is an economic methodology problem.

Living income methodology requires defining a household consumption basket — what a family of four needs to meet basic needs with dignity — and calculating the income required to afford it. For agricultural workers in Western UP, that meant looking at food prices in local mandis, housing costs in rural Barabanki, education and healthcare expenditure data from NSSO surveys, and then comparing against actual farmer earnings from Norex's FarmsIO data.

The result is not a single number but a methodology that Norex can defend to an EcoVadis auditor — showing the data sources, the assumptions, and the calculation logic. That defensibility is the actual product.

Status

The proposal covering all three systems was delivered to Norex leadership. The sustainability manager responded positively. The project is in discussion pending final approval. The economic diagnosis and system design are complete — the implementation begins once the engagement is confirmed.
