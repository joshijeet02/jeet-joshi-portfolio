Approach

How I think about problems

Economics taught me that every problem has a structure. Someone is bearing a cost. Some information is missing. Some incentive is pulling the wrong way. Once you find the structure, you can see what would actually change things.

Most AI tools are built without that kind of diagnosis. They are built for a general version of a problem — not for the specific situation this particular organization is in, with this particular data, this particular team, and these particular constraints.

The systems I build start with a diagnosis, not a demo. I spend time understanding the economics of the situation first. Only then do I build.

That process is slower. But the systems that come out of it tend to work.
