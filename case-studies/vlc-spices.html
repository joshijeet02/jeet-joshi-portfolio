Export Intelligence Dashboards — VLC Spices

Two system designs for an Indian spice exporter competing in the US, UK, and South African markets.

In Discussion Market EntryCompetitor Monitoring Export ComplianceAPI Integration

Context

VLC Spices is an Indian exporter shipping to international markets, primarily competing with other Indian suppliers for buyers in the US, Europe, and South Africa. Their current sales process relies on manual relationship-building, trade fairs, and static market research. They requested a system to operationalize trade data and automate competitor analysis.

The challenge is shifting from "who are the buyers?" to "which buyers are statistically likely to switch suppliers right now?" and operationalizing that into an actionable dashboard.

The Dual System Architecture

After detailed discussions, two distinct intelligence systems were designed to cover both the acquisition of new buyers and the operational defense of existing ones:

  • System One: Trade Intelligence Dashboard — A prospect generation and market monitoring tool. It tracks every shipment leaving India or entering the US. It ranks prospective buyers by relationship instability (e.g. buyers exhibiting erratic shipment histories or gaps with their current suppliers). Features an unserved buyer list, competitor defense alerts (when a client trials a new supplier), and one-click AI-drafted outreach based on trade history.
  • System Two: Export Documentation Intelligence — An operational compliance monitor. It tracks compliance requirements per product category per destination (USFDA, FSSC 22000, etc.) in real-time. It maps VLC's existing certifications against market requirements and automatically flags gaps before shipments are delayed at the border.

The Data Pipeline

Rather than relying entirely on expensive proprietary data, the system is designed to use a hybrid pipeline. It marries paid shipment-level data with free, aggregate sources to minimize running costs.

The dashboard runs on Volza for shipment-level buyer intelligence, integrated directly via API. However, five additional modules—Competitor Reports, Market Entry Scoring, Price Benchmarking, EU RASFF Monitor, and Spice Board/APEDA tracking—are built on free data sources like USITC, UN Comtrade, ITC MacMap, and ICEGATE. Because the data pipeline is already running, these modules are generated at zero incremental data cost for VLC.

This approach transforms raw customs data into a ranked daily action list. The dashboard stays open in a browser tab. The client does not run queries or export spreadsheets; they open it, see what matters today, and act.

Status

The detailed proposal encompassing the architecture, data sources, and explicit cost breakdowns has been submitted to VLC Spices. The project is currently in discussion pending final approval. The economic strategy and system design are complete; implementation begins upon engagement confirmation.
