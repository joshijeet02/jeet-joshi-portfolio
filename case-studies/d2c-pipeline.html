D2C Brand Outreach Pipeline

An automated pipeline that scrapes Instagram, scores engagement signals, and generates personalized cold emails — 30–40 brands per batch

DeployedApifyPythonSignal ScoringSales AutomationGmail API

What It Does

Manually researching and personalizing outreach to 30–40 D2C brands takes 2 hours per brand. The problem is not the writing — it is the research: understanding what the brand is doing on Instagram, which signals indicate a founder is looking for growth help, and what specific observation to lead with in the email.

This pipeline automates the research and drafts the emails. It takes a CSV of brand names and Instagram handles, scrapes their recent Instagram activity using Apify, scores the data for meaningful signals (comment anomaly patterns, reach spikes relative to follower count, friction signals that suggest stalled growth), generates a 180-word personalized cold email in Jeet's voice based on the specific brand's signals, and saves the draft directly to Gmail. Jeet reviews and sends — the research and writing are done.

The Signal Scoring Logic

The most interesting design problem was signal scoring. Not all engagement patterns mean the same thing. A reach spike on a product post means something different from a reach spike on a founder story. A high comment-to-like ratio on a complaint post is a different signal from the same ratio on a new product launch. The scorer was built with these distinctions baked in — because a generic "high engagement = good" rule generates irrelevant outreach. The economic logic is: lead with the signal that tells the founder you actually looked, not a generic compliment.
