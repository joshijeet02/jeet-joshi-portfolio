Voice AI Facilitator & Tribal Sovereignty Dashboard

Moving from a "Chatbot" to a "Trusted Village Sister" (Badi Behen) for the Bhil community in Rajasthan.

Pilot-Ready Hindi NLPSwaraj Framework OCR PipelineCommunity Governance Human-Centered AI

Context

VAAGDHARA works with Bhil tribal communities across Rajasthan, MP, and Gujarat. The challenge wasn't just providing information; it was providing it through a medium that felt safe and culturally congruent. Most AI tools sound like government officials or urban assistants — registers that often trigger hesitation or distance in a tribal context.

Mai Hun Saksham was redesigned to be a "Trusted Village Sister" (Badi Behen). It is built to facilitate KASS (Kissan Adivasi Swavlamban Sangathan) meetings, focusing on decentralized agency rather than top-down instruction.

The Core Pillars: Swaraj & True Life

The system is built on a structured knowledge framework derived from two decades of VAAGDHARA's field expertise:

  • The Swaraj Models — Deep intelligence on 5 forms of sovereignty: Jal (Water), Jameen (Land), Van (Forest), Beej (Seed), and Ann (Food).
  • True Life Frameworks — Conversational paths focused on True Farming (holistic agriculture), True Childhood (nutritional and community security), and True Governance (participatory decision-making).
  • Cultural Integrity — Codified definitions for community practices like HALMA (collective labor) and NYOTRA (social-economic co-operation) to ensure the AI understands the village's real social glue.

The Innovation: 4-Intelligence Scoring

Unlike standard QA systems, the app doesn't just listen; it evaluates community readiness. Every dialogue is mapped and scored (1-10) across four dimensions:

Ecological: Are we managing resources as a collective? Economic: Is risk-sharing happening at the village level? Civic: Does the agenda reflect female leadership? Cultural: Is the knowledge being passed to the next generation?

This allows VAAGDHARA to monitor the "pulse" of community agency across hundreds of villages simultaneously, identifying where leadership is thriving and where it needs support.

Technical Implementation

  • OCR Pipeline — 20 years of tribal knowledge were locked in paper newsletters. I built a Tesseract-based Hindi OCR pipeline with automated quality review to digitize and index these primary sources into the KB.
  • Voice Agent Persona — A pilot-ready prompt for platforms like Bland AI, explicitly instructed to speak in the register of a "Trusted Village Sister." It avoids "Do you know?" questions in favor of experience-based starts: "इस बार बारिश..." (This time the rain...).
  • Scored Dialogue Bank — A 200-question engine that powers the "Facilitator" mode, guiding women through sessions while calculating their intelligence scores in real-time.

Outcome

The system has moved from a document-retrieval concept to a community facilitation tool. It currently holds 200+ scored questions and 150+ synthesized practice definitions. It is ready for field pilots, designed to empower tribal women to lead their own village assemblies (Gram Sabhas) with the backing of their community's collective wisdom.
