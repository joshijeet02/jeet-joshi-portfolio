RBI Communication Intelligence

A Streamlit-powered intelligence platform that reads every Reserve Bank of India communication — MPC statements, Governor speeches, policy minutes — and tells you exactly how the central bank's language has shifted from one meeting to the next.

Context

Reserve Bank of India communications are where the country's monetary policy stance is set, signalled, and revised. A single word change in the MPC statement — "vigilant" replacing "watchful", or "durable alignment" appearing where "transitory" used to sit — can move the yield curve by basis points and reset banking sector expectations for the quarter.

The problem: that signal is buried in dense, jargon-heavy PDFs across MPC resolutions, Governor speeches, and meeting minutes. Analysts have to read every document twice — once to understand it, once more to compare it against the previous one. Most don't. The signal goes unread.

The RBI Communication Intelligence system was built to do that reading at machine speed — and to turn the language of monetary policy into a structured, queryable, comparable corpus.

System Architecture

The platform is built as a hybrid retrieval + reasoning system layered over a clean RBI corpus:

  • Automated Corpus Ingestion — A Playwright-driven scraper monitors the RBI's MPC and Governor's Speech index pages, ingests every new document as it is published, and chunks it into clean, citation-anchored passages using a custom semantic chunker.
  • Stance Signal Engine — A rule-based + LLM-assisted scorer reads every communication and produces a hawkish/dovish stance score, an inflation assessment, a growth assessment, a liquidity stance, and a forward-guidance read — each one auditable back to the underlying sentences.
  • Meeting-to-Meeting Comparison — The auto-briefing engine compares the latest MPC document to the previous one and surfaces exactly what changed: which terms appeared for the first time, which framings shifted, and how the overall stance moved on a continuous scale.
  • Dual-Mode Query Interface — Two audiences, two modes. Analyst mode returns full economist-grade output with citations and stance scores. Layman mode reframes every answer in plain English — what the RBI is saying about EMIs, prices, jobs, and your savings — with a jargon glossary one click away.
  • Claude-Powered Synthesis — The Anthropic Claude API handles the natural-language layer: query understanding, multi-document synthesis, and the layperson reframing that makes monetary policy accessible without dumbing it down.

What it answers

The system is designed to answer the questions that traders, treasury teams, and policy researchers actually ask between MPC meetings:

  • How has the RBI's stance on inflation shifted over the last four meetings?
  • What new focus terms appeared in this MPC statement that weren't in the last one?
  • What is the Governor signalling about liquidity withdrawal in the most recent speech?
  • In plain English — does this MPC outcome make home loan EMIs more or less likely to fall?

The Tech Stack

Python for the ingestion and signal engine. SQLite with FTS5 for the searchable corpus. Streamlit for the UI. Claude API for synthesis. Playwright for the scraping layer. The whole stack runs on Streamlit Cloud with deterministic, low-cost reruns.

Every claim the system makes is anchored to a passage in the source document. There are no free-floating LLM hallucinations — the retrieval layer always grounds the synthesis layer, and the corpus view lets you inspect any document the engine cited.

Status

The system is fully operational and deployed at rbicommunication.streamlit.app. It currently indexes MPC statements, Governor speeches, and policy minutes, with ongoing ingestion of new communications as they are published. It is designed as a companion to the India Macro Pulse system — where Macro Pulse handles the data releases (CPI, IIP, surprise indices), RBI Comms handles the language and stance that frames how those releases will be interpreted.
