The Lens: Agency as a Metric

The core philosophy of this system is to measure how empowered women feel they are. In tribal community development, progress isn't just about resource distribution; it's about the internal shift from being a passive participant to a community leader. This app codifies that shift through a "Leadership Arc" lens.

Currently deployed across the Bhil tribal belt in Rajasthan, MP, and Gujarat, the system handles real-time data from 120+ field facilitators covering 2,800+ life stories and leadership assessments.

The Engine: 5 Intelligences & The Ladder

The assessment tracks progress across five critical dimensions of agency:

  • Ecological & Economic — Resource sovereignty and risk-sharing.
  • Civic & Cultural — Voice in village governance and preservation of heritage.
  • Digital — Agency in navigating the digital governance and commerce landscape.

Women are mapped onto The Ladder of Leadership, moving through four rungs: Participant (भागीदार), Doer (कर्ता), Motivator (प्रेरक), and finally, Leader (नेता). Immediately after an assessment, the system provides "Current 3 Tasks"—concrete, AI-driven nudges to help the individual reach the next rung of their arc.

Technical Excellence: The Hybrid-Sync Architecture

Working in remote tribal locations requires extreme resilience. The system features a custom hybrid-sync mobile architecture built with React Native (Expo):

Online/Offline Resilience: Field workers can conduct extensive interviews in areas with zero connectivity. Data is stored in a structured local DB and automatically synced to the Vercel/FastAPI backend the moment the device reconnects.

The stack involves a FastAPI (Python) backend, a React-based monitoring dashboard, and a PostgreSQL database with 24-hour automated backups, ensuring that no community story is ever lost, regardless of field conditions.

Outcome & Scale

This is no longer a pilot. It is an operational communal intelligence engine. By mapping leadership at the household, village, and panchayat levels, VAAGDHARA can now visualize "Swaraj Signals"—real-time indicators of community agency that inform strategic decision-making across the entire belt.
