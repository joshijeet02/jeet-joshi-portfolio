04 About

I grew up in Banswara. I started building because I ran out of alternatives.

I trained at the Gokhale Institute of Politics and Economics, Pune and the University of Sydney. I spent years in policy research — government budgets, rural employment, economic rights — before I started building the systems I kept wishing existed.

The gap was always the same. The economic analysis existed. No system carried it forward. The insight would arrive in a report, and then the report would sit.

So I started building the systems myself.

I also write a newsletter called Chai with an Economist. About 3,000 people read it. I write when something has been sitting with me long enough that not writing it feels wrong.
